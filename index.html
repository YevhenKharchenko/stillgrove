The Grove

Stillgrove is not just a location. It is a memory that never fully settled. Trees stand where they always have, but something about them feels delayed. Light lingers longer than it should. Footsteps echo even after the walker has disappeared.
You may notice a branch moving — and then moving again, slightly differently. A figure crossing the same path twice, but not quite the same way. A sound that arrives before the action that caused it.
Everything here happens in layers of time. We’re not gonna spell it out for you — you’ll need to watch carefully and piece things together yourself. The deeper you go, the more you begin to understand: time here is not broken. It is layered.

The Echo Mechanic

Nothing Happens Only Once

Every action leaves a trace. In Stillgrove, those traces don’t disappear — they remain, quietly repeating themselves in the background.
These echoes are not decorations. They are part of the puzzle.
You might see your past self walking across a clearing. You might watch an object fall again and again, each time slightly out of sync. These repetitions are clues, tools, and sometimes obstacles.

Figuring out when these echoes show up is crucial. You’ll need to watch for patterns — when they appear, how they overlap, what happens when different versions of the same moment crash into each other.
This is not about reacting quickly. It is about noticing patterns that others would ignore.

Multiple Timelines

Several Realities, One Space

Usually time moves in a straight line, right? Not here. Think of it more like a crumpled piece of paper — folded, layered, with different parts touching each other in weird ways.
Different timelines exist simultaneously: one where an action has not yet happened, one where it is happening now, one where it has already passed.
You are allowed to move between them, but not freely. Each shift requires understanding. Each interaction creates consequences across layers.
A door opened in one timeline might remain closed in another. A path cleared in the past might not exist in the present — unless you align them correctly.
The puzzle is not just what to do. It is when.
